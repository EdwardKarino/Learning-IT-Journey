Legal Knowledge Management System (LKMS) - Kenya

Project Overview
This project aims to bridge the gap between complex legal statutes and the average Kenyan citizen. The "Kenyan Legal Knowledge Management System" is a platform designed to categorize, store, and simplify access to Kenyan Law, specifically focusing on the 2010 Constitution and key Acts of Parliament.

The Mission
In Kenya, access to legal information is often gatekept by complex jargon and scattered documentation. My goal is to build a "Single Source of Truth" that uses modern IT architecture to make the law searchable and understandable.

Technical Focus (The "IT" Side)
As an IT student, I am using this project to apply and "stress-test" the following concepts:
  Database Design:Implementing structured schemas for Acts, Sections, and Case Law.
  Knowledge Retrieval:Exploring semantic search and indexing to help users find relevant laws using natural language.
  Data Protection:Ensuring the system complies with the "Kenya Data Protection Act (2019)" regarding user queries and personal legal data.

Core Research Areas
Currently, I am researching how to integrate the following legal frameworks into the system:
1. The Constitution of Kenya (2010):Focus on Bill of Rights (Art. 19-59).
2. ICT & Cyber Law:Including the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act.
3. Data Sovereignty:Understanding how the National Digital Master Plan (2022-2032) affects legal tech in Kenya.
